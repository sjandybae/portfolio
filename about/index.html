About Andy Bae

At first glance, it is easy and understandable to look at my skills and consider me a generalist, a jack-of-all-trades. I would like to emphasize that I have had significant success with coding-related positions, but because my passion is in analyzing and constructing stories, I now work to utilize my skills and capabilities towards that end.

I am a writer who can also be a programmer.
I am a programmer who can also be a writer.

I see the greatest overlap between these fields, as well as the most intriguing, to be video games, if not games in general. When I see people designing gameplay, creating art, and building mechanics and systems, I want to use my knowledge on each of those fields to inform my writing and imbue the project with purpose.

I am a narrative designer.

Games are capable of creating immersive, compelling, and meaningful stories experienced through play. In other words, game narrative design goes beyond merely written dialogue; it is about using the interactive features of a game to create emotional ties to the world it creates. This is the role I am seeking, and fully qualified to fulfill.

Education

In 2020, I graduated Simon Fraser University with a Bachelor of Arts (with Distinction) in Interactive Arts and Technology, in which I learned a wide variety of technical skills applicable to many industries.

I completed three co-op work terms where I worked for web and game-related companies, and also studied Storytelling Across Media in an international exchange in Sweden.

Interests

Aside from video games, my hobbies include writing short stories, reading comics, watching anime, and sometimes remembering to practice kendo or the drums. During university, I also co-founded a manga club and helped manage its events and projects for 5 years.

About this site

This entire website was coded by me from scratch (i.e. without any template) using what I learned from my web developer work experiences. Tools used include Bootstrap and jQuery for front-end, Brackets as a coding platform, and GitHub for tracking and hosting. I found and edited royalty free images for the icons.
