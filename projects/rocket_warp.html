Rocket Warp

April 2014 | Endless Runner Game

Main Role: Game Designer + Programmer

#Processing #Java #GameDesign #OOP

  • Game Design

  • Graphic Design

  • Java

Download and play "Rocket Warp" here!

For an introductory game design class, one of the last assignments was to demonstrate all of the programming techniques we were taught in Java Processing, especially object-oriented programming and class inheritance.

"Rocket Warp" was my submission that not only utilized all of the concepts I learned, but also served as one of my first game design opportunities. I explored the core features of endlessly side-scrolling games, and balanced the subsystems around the idea of teleporting to avoid obstacles.

Core Loop

While dodging obstacles that spawn at random intervals and positions was the main concept, I decided that the player should have a consistent activity as well that would also be directly related to the goal of getting as far as possible. The player must keep collecting gems to keep their fuel from getting empty, which is also a lose condition.

Failing to collect gems was made to not be a significant penalty, however, as the player's primary concern should still be the random asteroids. To connect these two activities together, I had the warping ability consume a large amount of fuel for each use, incentivizing the player to collect enough gems to always have the warping ability available for sudden emergencies.

Difficulty and Options

Raising the difficulty the further the player got was an obvious decision, and from repeated testing, I landed on three difficulties, with higher difficulties being "faster" and more "dangerous". Gems would move faster and be harder to consistently collect, while the asteroid would appear more frequently, sometimes in large groups that effective force the player to use the warp abilitiy to avoid them.

Once I realized playing the highest difficulty was essentially just waiting to die from a sudden lucky asteroid, I decided to have power-ups rarely appear to offer some leeway in achieving a high score. One power-up allows the player to slow down time and pull off some narrow dodges, and the other gives a barrier that allows the player to afford one hit without dying.
