I love putting together games that create stories.

At SFU's School of Interactive Arts and Technology, I studied and practiced artistic expression through various technology. I made graphics, interactive films, board games, animations, documentaries, and (for this one class) a fake "high-tech" garbage can.

Through my experiences in work and play, nothing brings me greater joy than playing a fun, narrative-driven video game except for working with other skillful creators to make such projects. Games today embody our culture, ideals, and emotional experiences, and I want to be behind the scenes making lots of them.

I value friendliness in both coding and logistics.

Many of my past school and work experiences required me to teach myself new programs and languages regularly. I have had great success with full-stack web development, various Adobe software, Maya, Java, and Unity for VR games. I have had the opportunity to pass these skills on to many young children as well.

While I chose a path that would put my programming brain towards creative, entertainment-related media, I still enjoy solving problems and putting the pieces of a project together. Show me a team of game creators, and my skills can and will cover whatever gaps there may be.
