November 2018 | Videowalk

Main Role: Cameraman
Subroles: Video Editor, Script Writer

#VideoWalk #ImmersiveFilm #LiveActing #BeautifulSweden

  • Storytelling

  • Filmography

  • Editing

While studying abroad in Sweden, the project I am most proud to have worked on was the videowalk assignment, a short film intended to be watched on a mobile device while the viewer is walking through the exact location being explored in the film itself.

Our film concept capitalized on this "spot-the-difference" experience by having the film be set about one year in the future, guided by a protagonist who needs the viewer's help to investigate his sister's disappearance from her office a year ago (i.e. the viewer's present year).

Within the couple of weeks we were given, our team of both international and local students hosted auditions for aspiring actors to play our characters both in the film and during the videowalk, the latter of which required live acting performed repeatedly for each viewer. I was in charge of the camera during the filming of "the future", utilizing an ear-worn omnidirectional mic for spatially accurate surround sound as I did the walk all in one continuous take.

The project was well received for its twist ending, especially with the live acting after the end of the video genuinely scaring some of our classmates and instructors. It was a particularly enlightening experience for me, writing dialogue for real actors to perform, and see our diverse team come together with their respective cultures' ideas on film and entertainment.

Shelter

December 2017 | "Serious" Board Game

Main Role: Game Designer
Subroles: Graphic Designer

#BoardGame #Homelessness #GameDesign #RulesheetDesign

  • Game Design

  • Leadership

  • Graphic Design

  • Design Docs

All of the projects for this one game design class I took were board games, the last one being a board game to utilize "serious fun", coined by Nicole Lazzaro, and essentially create an altered state and changed perspective in people through play. "Shelter" is a board game three peers and I created where players could experience their own unique struggles and narratives trying to survive as a homeless person.

Since board games are completely driven by the players, absolute clarity in the rule sheet (made using Adobe Illustrator) and several balance tweaks were required to make sure players were certain of their decisions and feel "fun" in what would normally be a dreary situation. Based on playtesting before and after its implementation, we confirmed the Event card system really helped to make the game loop more exciting as well as more narrative-focused.

Norquay Food Hub - Fresh Roots

December 2017 | Short Documentary

Main Role: Cameraman
Subroles: Video Editor, Storyboard Artist

#Documentary #Filming #Editing #NonProfitOrganization

  • Filmography

  • Editing

Working in a team of four, our major assignment for a film course was to develop a 5 minute documentary for a non-profit organization, ours being Fresh Roots and its headquarters at Norquay Neighbourhood Food Hub. We collaborated closely with our client, the executive director of Fresh Roots, to ensure our film and footage would meet our assignment criteria of "telling a story" as well as market the goals of the organization.

After some storyboard work, we attended two of Fresh Roots's events to film B-roll footage and gather interviews with some of the volunteers. After also receiving some B-roll footage from our client, my skills with video editing and I were at the center of a deep, collaborative crunch as our entire team discussed all of the cuts, transitions, interview dialogue, and post-processing needed to bring our film to a professional level of quality.

Tiny Planet

August 2016 | 3D Animation

Main Role: Director + Project Manager
Subroles: Animator, Storyboard Artist, Voice Actor

#3DAnimation #AnimatedShort #Maya #PhotoShop #AfterEffects#PremierePro

  • Storytelling

  • Leadership

  • Animation

  • Direction

  • Graphic Design

  • Editing

"Tiny Planet" is a 3D animated short featuring an alien that is significantly larger than its fellow aliens, which causes trouble to their working society on a very tiny planet. I acted as the project leader for my team of four during the entire 3 month process, including drawing initial storyboards and creating an animatic.

With such a tight timeline for work, I had to quickly set the scope and assign tasks to each team member based on our respective skills. Most of my work was in the actual animations after my teammates finished the assets and rigging, so I also took on the task of editing together the final video.

Time constraints led to some of the minor details and animation cuts being done in Adobe After Effects and PhotoShop. I also had to resolve conflicts in the team's individual schedules by reassigning tasks and keeping everyone motivated. Nevertheless, the film was very well received by our class and instructor for the touching story, cute animations, and hilarious voice acting.

The Hidden Scoop

April 2016 | Interactive Documentary

Main Role: Film Director
Subroles: Cameraman, Schedule Manager, Video Editor

#InteractiveFilm #BranchingPaths #FilmDirection #YouTube#MultilinearNarrative

  • Storytelling

  • Leadership

  • Filmography

  • Direction

  • Editing

NOTE: All of the YouTube annotations broke since the 21 videos were first uploaded in 2016, so the featured video is the secret bad ending where the protagonist and cameraman are betrayed and murdered.

"The Hidden Scoop" is a multilinear narrative project featuring a brazen young journalist creating a documentary series to uncover the secrets of a missing (fictional) city official. Our plan was to use YouTube annotations to connect videos to other videos, and so we brainstormed several paths and endings that could offer completely different answers surrounding the official's disappearance.

With careful editing, many videos were made to be interpreted by the viewer differently depending on which videos led them to these ones, making a unique, organic experience for each playthrough. Since the viewer is making decisions for the story, the actors sometimes directly interact with the "cameraman" of this fictional film crew, usually to ask which Skytrain station to visit next (since we were commuting for free with our student passes).

Protii

December 2014 | Interactive Art Installation

Main Role: Programmer

#InteractiveArt #CommunityArt #Java #Programming #ArtGallery

  • Leadership

  • Java

  • Design Docs

As part of an Interactive Art course, I led a team of peers in researching community art, and utilized my experience in Java programming to make a creation interface for customizable avatars as well as their behaviours in our "digital art installation". I used the Eclipse IDE and the ControlP5 library to set up the interactive interface on a runnable laptop app. The graphical options were created by my teammates.

We named the avatars "Protii", deriving from the word "protean", and the project truly became an example of community art at the class's open showcase where we had around 40 people create and enter their own Protiis, each with their own distinct combination of parts, colours, and personalities. Visitors passing by could see the avatars' unique greetings pop up, inviting them to signal questions for the Protii to answer and, in many cases, create their own Protii.

Main Role: Animator
Subroles: Storyboard Artist, Video Editor

#KineticTypography #Animation #Editing #AdobeAfterEffects

  • Animation

  • Direction

  • Editing

NOTE: The video's audio belongs to Audio Adrenaline and related parties. I do not possess any form of ownership over the song.

One of the major assignments for a Digital Image Design course was to produce kinetic typography, an animation that uses primarily words. I used "Believer" by Audio Adrenaline for its descriptive lyrics, and had a lot of fun using Adobe After Effects and focusing purely on words and effects without worrying about asset creation.

The final product very closely follows the very detailed storyboard I drew. Despite this project being my first exposure to After Effects and technical art techniques such as particle systems, my work was very well received by my instructor, who urged me to consider pursuing animation as a career.
